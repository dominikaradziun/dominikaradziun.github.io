I am a computational cognitive neuroscientist and Postdoctoral Researcher at the [Brain, Body, and Technology Lab](https://www.brainbodytech.com/) at the Donders Institute, Radboud University. My research asks how the brain builds models of the body and the world, and how these models change when sensory experience changes.

So far, I have studied this question through body perception, blindness, and sensorimotor augmentation: how blind and sighted individuals perceive their bodies, how the brain adapts when vision is absent, and how body representations change when we use tools or wearable devices. To study these questions, I combine behavioral experiments, neuroimaging, and computational modeling.

I was recently awarded an F.R.S.-FNRS Chargée de recherches fellowship at UCLouvain, where I will study why congenital blindness may protect against schizophrenia. This project will ask whether blindness leads to a distinctive, resilient way of combining prior expectations with sensory evidence, and whether this can help us understand vulnerability and resilience in psychosis.

I completed my PhD at Karolinska Institutet, where I investigated body perception and brain plasticity in blind and sighted individuals. My doctoral thesis was awarded the [Glushko Dissertation Prize](https://cognitivesciencesociety.org/glushko-dissertation-prize/) from the Cognitive Science Society. You can watch my Glushko Prize talk [here](https://underline.io/events/465/sessions/18086/lecture/99279-body-perception-and-brain-plasticity-in-blind-and-sighted-individuals).

Originally from Poland, my academic journey has taken me through Norway, Sweden, United Kingdom, Netherlands, and now Belgium. As a scientist, I feel privileged to ask big questions and mentor students as they grow into researchers themselves.
